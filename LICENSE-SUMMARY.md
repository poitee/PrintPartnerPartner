## What “noncommercial” means here

PolyForm allows use for **any noncommercial purpose**. In practice that includes:

- Personal use, hobbies, and club projects
- Research, experimentation, and learning
- Use by schools, charities, government, and similar organizations (even when funded)

PolyForm defines noncommercial more specifically in the **Noncommercial Purposes** and **Noncommercial Organizations** sections of [LICENSE](LICENSE). Uses that are **primarily for commercial advantage or monetary compensation** for the **software itself** are not covered.

## What you are allowed to do

- **Run** Print Partner on your own machines for noncommercial purposes.
- **Study and modify** the source code.
- **Share** the app or your modifications, but only if recipients get the same [LICENSE](LICENSE) terms and required copyright notice.
- **Use the app in a print business** — organizing kits, exporting 3MF, running checkoff — when you are **not** selling Print Partner or a fork as a product. You are using it as a tool; PolyForm restricts commercial exploitation of the **software**, not the physical parts you print and sell.

---

## What you are not allowed to do without permission

You need a **separate commercial agreement** (see [COMMERCIAL.md](COMMERCIAL.md)) to:

- Sell Print Partner or a modified version
- Bundle it in a paid product you distribute
- Offer it as a hosted or managed service customers pay for
- Use it in ways that do not qualify as noncommercial under PolyForm

**Ko-fi tips** are voluntary support. They **do not** grant commercial license rights.

## Forks and redistribution

If you pass copies or changes to someone else:

1. Include the [LICENSE](LICENSE) text (or the official URL).
2. Keep the **Required Notice** line that appears at the top of LICENSE.
3. Do not imply Chad Lynch endorses your fork unless you have permission.

That is how PolyForm implements a **share-alike** effect: downstream copies stay under the same noncommercial terms.
