## What “non-commercial” means here

**Non-commercial** in this license name refers to the **software**, not to whether you run a printing business. You may **not** sell or commercially redistribute Print Partner itself without permission. You **may** sell the **physical parts and print services** you produce with it.

The license allows use for **personal, academic, and non-commercial hobby** purposes. In practice that includes:

- Personal use, hobbies, and club projects
- Research, experimentation, and learning
- Running the app in your shop to organize kits, manifests, and STL exports

### Commercial print farms and print businesses

If you operate a **commercial** 3D printing business, print farm, or contract print shop, you may use Print Partner **internally** to run that business — plate planning, checkoff, manifests, exports, and similar workflow — **without** a separate commercial license, as long as you are **not** selling or hosting the **software** itself.

Examples of permitted **Commercial Print Farm Use**:

- A print farm using Print Partner on its own machines to fulfill paid print jobs
- A shop selling printed kits or parts that uses the app for production planning and QC
- Contract manufacturers organizing STL plates and checkoff for paying customers

You still need written permission from Chad Lynch to **commercialize the software** (installers, forks, SaaS, paid redistribution, bundling in a product you sell). See [COMMERCIAL.md](COMMERCIAL.md).
